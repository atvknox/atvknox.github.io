About me

I'm a student from Asia who spends way too much time experimenting with Linux distributions, ricing Hyprland, breaking Android phones, and making hardware question its existence.

My interests and hobbies include:

  • Linux systems administration & kernel tinkering
  • Experimenting with artificial intelligence and transformer stacks
  • Porting alternative operating systems onto unsupported mobile devices
  • Breaking things intentionally to figure out how they function under the hood
  • Naturally, cats.

Projects & Focus

Recently, I've been trying to train a transformer stack called MARJARA. Computing attention weights on my local system pushes the hardware limits, making my laptop fans sound like a jet engine taking off, but watching the training loss drop makes it entirely worth it.

Other projects include setting up a custom Hackintosh setup on an AMD Ryzen architecture and successfully deploying Ubuntu Touch onto an aging Xiaomi Redmi 7A.

The Workspace

My main environment is driven by an HP 247 G8 Notebook PC with 6GB usable RAM, an AMD Ryzen 5 3500U processor, and a Vega 8 iGPU. It runs a minimal, dual-boot environment containing custom-tailored Arch Linux and macOS 12 on a 512GB NVMe SSD.
